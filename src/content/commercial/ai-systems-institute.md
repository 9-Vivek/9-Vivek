The AI Systems Institute (ASI) is a learning platform designed to move students from using AI tools toward understanding and designing complete AI systems. Its initial focus is making systems thinking accessible to high-school students without reducing the subject to vocabulary quizzes, disconnected code exercises, or prompt techniques.

## Purpose

ASI teaches students to reason about boundaries, data movement, failure modes, model tradeoffs, operational constraints, and human oversight. The goal is not simply to introduce machine-learning concepts. It is to help students develop the habits required to plan and explain how an AI-enabled product works as a system.

The curriculum is organized around connected technical artifacts. Each module contributes one structured piece of a larger design, and each level ends with an integration sprint that combines those pieces into a portfolio-grade system specification or implementation.

## What I am building

The platform combines four elements:

- Short conceptual briefings that establish an intuitive system model.
- Interactive systems drills that make tradeoffs and failure conditions visible.
- A scaffolded workspace for producing technical artifacts rather than answering isolated questions.
- A Socratic AI coach that reviews the student's current work, identifies structural gaps, and asks guiding questions without producing the answer for them.

The first release focuses on Level 1: AI Systems Strategy. Its platform architecture includes a progress dashboard, a focused builder workspace, structured module progression, automated artifact review, and a public registry where completed work can be inspected as evidence of capability.

## Longer-term goal

ASI is intended to provide a progression from conceptual systems strategy into data analysis and production integration. As the levels advance, the artifacts become more technical and implementation-oriented, but the learning model remains the same: understand a system, manipulate its constraints, produce a component, and integrate that component into a larger whole.

The central objective is to make serious AI systems education active, cumulative, and professionally legible.

## Big-picture position

ASI is adjacent to the ACC research rather than a direct implementation of it. Both share an emphasis on active cognition, visible structure, and human agency, but ASI applies those principles to education: the learner constructs understanding through connected artifacts and guided reflection. Within the professional portfolio, it demonstrates product architecture, curriculum systems design, AI-assisted evaluation, and the ability to translate complex technical ideas into an accessible user-facing platform.
